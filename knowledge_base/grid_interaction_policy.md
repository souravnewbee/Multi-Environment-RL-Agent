# Grid Interaction Policy
UMORDA Energy Domain — Grid Energy Buying and Selling Optimization

## Self-Sufficiency Priority
The default goal is energy self-sufficiency — using the home's own solar generation and battery storage to cover all consumption without grid involvement. Self-sufficiency reduces electricity bills, reduces grid dependency, and maximizes the benefit of the balcony solar panel investment.

## Selling to the Grid
Surplus solar energy beyond home consumption and battery capacity should be sold to the grid, particularly when grid prices are high (price level 2). Selling during expensive periods maximizes financial return from surplus generation. Selling when price is cheap or normal (price level 0 or 1) returns less value and may not be worth the effort.

## Selling Criteria
- Solar surplus must be greater than 0 to sell. Never attempt to sell with zero surplus.
- Higher surplus combined with higher grid price produces the best selling opportunity.
- Surplus of 4 or more with price level 2 is an ideal selling situation.

## Buying from the Grid
Buying from the grid is justified only when own resources (solar surplus and battery) are fully depleted and home consumption cannot be met. When buying is unavoidable, it should be done during cheap price periods (price level 0) to minimize cost.

## Grid Price Decision Guide
- Grid price 0 (cheap): acceptable to buy if needed, poor time to sell.
- Grid price 1 (normal): buy only if necessary, sell only if large surplus exists.
- Grid price 2 (expensive): avoid buying entirely, excellent time to sell surplus.

## Avoiding Unnecessary Grid Purchase
Buying from the grid when solar surplus or battery charge exists is a policy violation. Own energy resources must always be exhausted before purchasing grid electricity. Grid purchase when own resources are available represents unnecessary cost.

## Self-Sufficiency Assessment
The home is self-sufficient when solar surplus plus battery level covers home consumption. In this case, neither buying nor selling is needed and the agent should stay self-sufficient to preserve own resources.
