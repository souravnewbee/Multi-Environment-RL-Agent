# Emergency Room Triage Policy

## Priority Rule
Emergency patients must always be served before normal-queue patients
whenever the emergency queue is non-empty. This is a strict priority
rule grounded in standard ER triage protocol: life-threatening cases
take precedence regardless of how long normal-queue patients have
waited.

## Serving the Normal Queue
The normal queue should only be served when the emergency queue is
completely empty. Serving normal-queue patients while emergency cases
are waiting is considered a serious triage failure and increases risk
to critical patients.

## Queue Backlog Risk
As the emergency queue grows, the urgency of clearing it increases
non-linearly. A backlog of more than 5 emergency patients indicates a
resource strain event and emergency processing should be treated as
the only acceptable action until the backlog clears.

## Normal Queue Management
Once the emergency queue is cleared, normal patients should be served
promptly to prevent their condition from escalating into an emergency
case, which would further strain ER resources.
