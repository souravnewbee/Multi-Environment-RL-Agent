# Bed Allocation Policy

## Admission Criteria
Patients should be admitted whenever free beds are available, prioritizing
admission over transfer when bed capacity exceeds 5 beds. Admitting early
prevents the waiting queue from growing and reduces patient risk.

## Transfer Criteria
When free beds drop to 5 or fewer, the hospital should begin transferring
new patients to partner facilities to preserve remaining capacity for
critical or already-waiting cases. Transfer is the correct action when
zero beds remain, since admission is not physically possible.

## Rejection Policy
Rejecting a patient outright is against hospital policy under almost all
circumstances. Even at full capacity, patients should be transferred to
another facility rather than turned away with no alternative arranged.
Rejection should only be considered a last resort when both beds and
transfer capacity are exhausted.

## Capacity Risk Thresholds
- More than 10 free beds: low risk, admit freely.
- 5 to 10 free beds: moderate risk, admit but monitor closely.
- Fewer than 5 free beds: high risk, prefer transfer.
- 0 free beds: critical, transfer is mandatory.

## Patient Load Considerations
A high number of waiting patients combined with low bed availability
signals a capacity crisis. In these cases, transfer protects both the
incoming patient and those already admitted from overcrowding-related
risk.
