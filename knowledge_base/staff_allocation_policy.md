# Staff Allocation Policy

## Staffing Ratio Guidelines
Patient load should be matched to available doctors using a balanced
ratio. A patient load under 10 indicates overstaffing relative to
demand. A patient load between 10 and 30 indicates balanced staffing.
A patient load above 30 indicates understaffing and requires
additional doctors.

## Assigning Additional Staff
More staff should be assigned when patient load exceeds 30, since
delays in care become likely beyond this threshold. Assigning staff
when load is below 15 is considered wasteful and increases operating
cost without improving patient outcomes.

## Reducing Staff
Reducing staff is appropriate only when patient load is low, generally
below 10. Reducing staff while load remains moderate or high is
considered dangerous, since it risks delayed care for patients already
in the system. Hospitals should never reduce staff under high-load
conditions even temporarily.

## Maintaining Current Staffing
Keeping the current staffing level is the correct action when patient
load falls within the balanced range of 10 to 30, since both adding
and removing staff in this range would be suboptimal relative to
actual demand.

## Risk of Understaffing
Chronic understaffing under sustained high patient load is associated
with increased patient wait times and degraded care quality, and
should be corrected as soon as load trends upward.
