# Parking Lot Management Policy
UMORDA Traffic Domain — Parking Lot Entry Management

## Capacity Guidelines
- Available spots 8 to 19: low occupancy, open entry freely to maximize usage.
- Available spots 4 to 7: moderate occupancy, open zone based on incoming vehicle load.
- Available spots 1 to 3: high occupancy risk, prefer Zone B to preserve remaining capacity.
- Available spots 0: lot is full, close entry immediately to prevent congestion.

## Zone Selection Criteria
Zone A should be opened when incoming vehicle load is high and sufficient spots are available to absorb the demand. Zone B is preferred when the lot is moderately full and incoming load is manageable, as it preserves more capacity for future arrivals. Closing entry is only justified when the lot is at or near full capacity.

## Queue Wait Time Thresholds
- Queue wait 0 to 2 steps: normal, no urgency to change current action.
- Queue wait 3 to 5 steps: moderate queue buildup, open a zone to relieve congestion.
- Queue wait 6 to 7 steps: high congestion risk, force open Zone A if spots exist.
- Queue wait 8 or more steps: critical congestion, safety override opens entry if any spots remain.

## Closing Entry Policy
Closing entry should only be done when the lot is genuinely full or nearly full. Closing entry unnecessarily when spots remain causes vehicles to queue outside, creates street congestion, and frustrates drivers. Premature closure is a policy violation unless spots are 2 or fewer.

## Overflow Risk Management
When incoming vehicles exceed available spots, the agent should open the zone that best absorbs the current load while minimizing overflow. If overflow is unavoidable, close entry early to prevent further congestion rather than letting vehicles enter and find no space.

## Occupancy Optimization
The goal is to keep occupancy high without causing congestion. An empty lot is wasted capacity. A full lot with queuing vehicles causes external congestion. The optimal state is 75 to 95 percent occupancy with no queue buildup.
